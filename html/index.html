Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about booking, payments, travel requirements, and more.

What documents do I need for international travel?

For international travel, you'll typically need a valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your return date. Many countries also require visas, which we can help you obtain. Always check the specific entry requirements for your destination at least 3 months before departure.

What is your cancellation policy?

Our cancellation policy varies depending on the package and supplier terms. Generally, cancellations made more than 60 days before departure receive a full refund minus a $200 administrative fee. Between 30-60 days, 50% of the package price is refundable. Within 30 days, packages are non-refundable. We strongly recommend purchasing travel insurance to protect your investment.

Do you offer travel insurance?

Yes, we partner with leading travel insurance providers to offer comprehensive coverage options. Our plans typically include trip cancellation/interruption, medical expenses, baggage loss/delay, and emergency evacuation. Insurance must be purchased within 14 days of your initial deposit to qualify for pre-existing condition coverage.

Can I customize my travel package?

Absolutely! Our travel specialists can customize any package to suit your preferences, whether you want to extend your stay, upgrade accommodations, add special experiences, or combine destinations. Customizations may affect the package price, and we'll always provide a detailed quote before confirming any changes.

What health precautions should I take before traveling?

Health requirements vary by destination. We recommend consulting with a travel medicine specialist 6-8 weeks before departure. Common precautions include vaccinations (like yellow fever or typhoid), malaria prophylaxis, and advice about food/water safety. The CDC's travel health website is an excellent resource for destination-specific recommendations.
