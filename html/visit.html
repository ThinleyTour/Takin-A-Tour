Quick Information

Visa Requirements

Check if you need a visa before traveling. Most visitors can stay up to 90 days visa-free.

Public Transport

Efficient metro and bus system. Get a travel card for unlimited rides during your stay.

Safety Tips

Generally safe, but beware of pickpockets in crowded areas. Emergency number: 112

Local Language

English is widely spoken. Learn basic greetings like "Hello" (Hola) and "Thank you" (Gracias).

Travel Checklist

Before You Travel

  • Check passport validity (6+ months remaining)
  • Purchase travel insurance
  • Notify your bank about travel plans
  • Download offline maps and translation apps

What to Pack

  • Universal power adapter
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Season-appropriate clothing (layers recommended)
  • Basic first aid kit and medications

Local Laws & Etiquette

Important Laws

  • Drinking in Public

    Allowed in most areas except near schools and churches.

  • Smoking

    Prohibited in all indoor public spaces. Fines up to €300.

  • Photography

    Avoid taking photos of military installations or government buildings.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Greetings

    A handshake is common. Close friends may greet with kisses on both cheeks.

  • Dining

    Keep hands visible on the table during meals. It's polite to try everything served.

  • Tipping

    10% is customary in restaurants if service isn't included. Round up taxi fares.

Currency & Exchange

EUR (€)
Euro

Official currency is the Euro. Some businesses may accept major credit cards, but cash is preferred in smaller establishments.

Exchange Tips

  • Best rates at banks and official exchange offices
  • Avoid exchanging at airports/hotels (higher fees)
  • Notify your bank before using cards abroad

Most shops open around 9-10 AM and close for siesta between 2-5 PM, then reopen until 8-9 PM. Larger stores and malls stay open all day. Restaurants typically serve lunch from 1-4 PM and dinner from 8 PM until midnight. Breakfast is available in cafes from 7 AM onwards.
